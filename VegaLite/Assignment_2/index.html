Interactive Visual Exploration of Australian Airports and Flight Routes

This interactive map of Australia illustrates the network of major airport routes across the country. Each airport is represented as a circle, with the size of the circle indicating the number of routes departing from that airport. The larger circles represent major hubs, showcasing their significance in air travel. The connecting lines reveal the routes between airports, highlighting the density and reach of the travel connections. This visualisation allows for an in-depth understanding of airport connectivity, offering insights into the busiest air travel nodes across Australia. Hover over any circle to view specific details such as the city's name, IATA code, and the number of routes available from that airport.

Exploring Australian Airports by State

This interactive visualisation provides a detailed overview of airports across Australia's major states and territories, including Victoria, New South Wales, Queensland, and more. Users can explore airports by selecting different states, and each airport is visually represented by its type and traffic volume. Larger circles denote airports with higher traffic, while different colours indicate airport types: red for large airports, orange for medium airports, and blue for small airports. Additionally, graticules are overlaid on the map to give a geographical reference grid, which adds context to the visualisation. The insights gained from this visualisation can help understand the distribution and connectivity of airports, especially in key hubs like Sydney and Melbourne.
